## üìñ Background

### What is ESG?

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) criteria are standards for evaluating a company's sustainability and ethical impact. These standards help organizations:
- Manage operational and reputational risks
- Comply with evolving regulations
- Build trust with stakeholders
- Demonstrate commitment to ethical operations
- Enhance long-term resilience

### Why ESG Matters for Banks

**Growing Importance**:
- Increasing regulatory pressure globally
- Rising investor expectations
- Consumer preference for ethical banking
- Risk management imperative
- Competitive differentiation

**Business Benefits**:
- Enhanced reputation and brand value
- Access to sustainable finance markets
- Lower cost of capital
- Improved risk management
- Attraction and retention of talent
- Long-term resilience

### The Three ESG Pillars

#### üåç Environmental
Focuses on a company's impact on the planet.

**Key Areas**:
- Carbon emissions and climate strategy
- Renewable energy usage
- Waste management and recycling
- Water conservation
- Green financing initiatives
- Climate risk mitigation

**Metrics**:
- Carbon footprint (tons CO2e)
- % Renewable energy consumption
- Waste diversion rate
- Water usage efficiency
- Green loan portfolio size

#### üë• Social
Examines impact on people and communities.

**Key Areas**:
- Workforce diversity and inclusion
- Employee health and safety
- Training and development
- Community engagement
- Corporate social responsibility (CSR)
- Labor practices

**Metrics**:
- Gender diversity %
- Minority representation %
- Training hours per employee
- CSR investment as % of profits
- Employee satisfaction scores
- Community investment

#### ‚öñÔ∏è Governance
Assesses leadership, controls, and ethics.

**Key Areas**:
- Board composition and diversity
- Internal controls and compliance
- Data security and privacy
- Risk management frameworks
- Shareholder rights
- Ethics and transparency

**Metrics**:
- Board diversity %
- Independent director ratio
- Cybersecurity incidents
- Compliance breaches
- Whistleblower program effectiveness
- Audit quality

### Task 5: RAROC Calculation

Calculate the Risk-Adjusted Return on Capital for each company's ESG initiatives.

#### Step 1: Calculate Risk-Adjusted Headline Earnings

**Formula**:
```
Risk-Adjusted Headline Earnings = Operating Income - Total Expenses - Expected Losses
```

**For Green Horizon Bank**:
```
Operating Income = ?
Total Expenses = R8 million (given)
Expected Losses = 0 (already accounted for)

RAHE = Operating Income - R8,000,000 - 0
```

**For Blue Earth Finance**:
```
Operating Income = R8.4 billion (given, losses accounted for)
Total Expenses = R7.9 billion (given)
Expected Losses = 0 (already accounted for)

RAHE = R8,400,000,000 - R7,900,000,000 = R500,000,000
```

#### Step 2: Calculate RAROC

**Formula**:
```
RAROC = Risk-Adjusted Headline Earnings / Total Allocated Capital
```

**For Green Horizon Bank**:
```
RAROC = R800,000,000 / R1,050,000,000 = 76.19%
```

**For Blue Earth Finance**:
```
RAROC = R500,000,000 / R850,000,000 = 58.82%
```

#### Step 3: Interpret Results

**Benchmark**: RAROC above 15% is considered good for banks.

**Analysis Questions**:
1. Which company has higher RAROC?
2. Are both above the 15% threshold?
3. What explains the difference in RAROC?
4. What does this mean for ESG investment decisions?

#### What Drives High or Low RAROC?

**High RAROC Indicators**:
- Strong revenue generation
- Efficient cost management
- Lower expected losses
- Optimal capital allocation
- High-quality risk management
- Strategic ESG investments with returns

**Low RAROC Indicators**:
- Weak revenue streams
- High operating costs
- Elevated expected losses
- Excessive capital allocation
- Poor risk management
- ESG investments without financial return

**Company-Specific Analysis**:

For **Green Horizon Bank**:
- Why is RAROC 76.19%?
- What are the key drivers?
- Is this sustainable?

For **Blue Earth Finance**:
- Why is RAROC 58.82%?
- What are the constraints?
- How can it be improved?

**Deliverable**:
- RAROC calculations for both companies
- Comparative analysis
- Explanation of high/low RAROC factors
- Strategic implications
