## üìñ Background

### What is Operational Risk?

According to the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision, operational risk is defined as:

> "The risk of loss resulting from inadequate or failed internal processes, people and systems from external events that disrupt business operations."

**Common Causes**:
- Employee errors
- Criminal and fraudulent activity
- Natural disasters
- Climate change impacts
- System failures

### Basel Risk Types Under Operational Risk
- **CPBP**: Clients, Products and Business Practices
- **DPA**: Damage to Physical Assets
- **EF**: External Fraud
- **IF**: Internal Fraud

### Employee Attrition Context

Employee attrition has become a growing concern for South African banks, including Nedbank. Key factors:
- Remote and hybrid work models are now the norm
- Teams demonstrate consistent productivity regardless of location
- Shifting employee expectations toward greater flexibility
- Pressure to retain valuable talent in competitive market
